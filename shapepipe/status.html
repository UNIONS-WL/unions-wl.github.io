§ reconvolution-PSF A/B — the largest lever

Switching the metacal reconvolution kernel from fitgauss to gauss sizing brings ⟨R1⟩ into the ±0.03 band, where fitgauss sat outside it: wcs +0.0405 → +0.0189 (−53%), hsm +0.0437 → +0.0136 (−69%). The catastrophic-fit tail (|R|>1) shrinks in step: 26% → 18%.

Mechanism. Both arms build a round reconvolution kernel; they differ only in how its size is set. fitgauss sizes it from a per-object adaptive-moments (admom) fit of the PSF stamp — fragile on noisy or tiny stamps, where the fit blows up and the metacal response runs away. gauss sizes it from a deterministic k-space match to the PSF image — robust, so the runaway tail collapses. It is not a Bayesian-prior effect; it is the kernel-sizing method.

The residual tail is the point-source floor. gauss removes the fixable fragility, but a 18% tail of catastrophic fits remains: the ellipticity response is singular as the pre-PSF intrinsic size T→0, and stars are point sources. It is exactly the regime a resolution cut excludes from a shear sample, so a residual ⟨R⟩ on a pure-star sample is a diagnostic of metacal’s point-source behaviour, not a direct shear bias.

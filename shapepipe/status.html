§ the test

What it is. The per-object metacal shear response R = dg/dγ measured on stars over 20 < mag < 26, across 9 tiles, with delete-one-tile jackknife errors. A round PSF-like object should give R = 0. Run as an A/B over the metacal reconvolution-PSF kernel sizing (fitgauss vs gauss), each crossed with the centroid source (wcs vs hsm).

What it measures. The mean per-object shear response of stars after metacal PSF deconvolution, and how it responds to the reconvolution-kernel-sizing choice.

Pass criteria. ⟨R1⟩ and ⟨R2⟩ each within ±0.03 of 0 (jackknife error).

§ the test

What it is. The per-object metacal response R = dg/dγ on the same SKiLLS sims for point-source stars (res < 0.06) and resolved galaxies (res > 0.4), binned in S/N. Resolution res = T/(T+Tpsf): 0 at the point-source limit, 1 fully resolved.

What it measures. Whether the resolved galaxies that carry the cosmic-shear signal inherit the star point-source-limit response climb, or sit in the well-behaved regime.

Pass criteria. At the highest S/N, resolved-galaxy ⟨R1⟩ ≈ ⟨R2⟩ (symmetric, |Δ| < 0.03) and ⟨R⟩ < 1 (below the point-source singularity).

§ Tier 1 — resolution × noise breakdown grid </>run_breakdown_grid.py
§ the method

Each grid cell is one self-contained GalSim simulation run in memory: an exponential galaxy convolved with a Moffat (β = 2.5) PSF, sampled at 0.1857″ pixels, with a known shear injected. The stamp is passed through the live pipeline shear estimator (do_ngmix_metacal), the same code the real survey uses, and the recovered shear, the metacal response R, and the additive bias c are read back out. Repeating this over N random seeds per cell and averaging gives the multiplicative bias m, the response R11, and the additive bias c1 for that cell. The grid sweeps two axes independently: galaxy size relative to the PSF (the resolution ratio r) and image noise (three signal-to-noise bands). The result is a map of where the calibration stays within tolerance and where it breaks down.
